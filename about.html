The Work &
The Workshop

Eastern Ontario. 16.5 acres. 45+ years of code.

The Property

16.5 acres of mixed forest south of Ottawa in Eastern Ontario. About 100 of those trees are sugar maples, currently running around 300 taps. The sugarhouse is on the property. The electronics workshop is too.

The maple operation and the embedded systems work share more than a location — SapMaster exists specifically to automate the physical infrastructure of sap collection. Every sensor and state machine has a direct counterpart in the sugarbush.

The boiler is a 24×48×8" stainless pan over a fire brick firebox built into the bottom half of a residential oil tank. Wood-fired. No automation. Syrup is drawn at the end of the day, not continuously.

The Background

45+ years of BASIC programming — from early microcomputers through BASCOM-AVR to Great Cow BASIC on the MEGA2560 today. The SapMaster codebase started as an 8,000+ line BASCOM project before migrating to GCB.

PCB design and fabrication is handled in-house. Boards are designed in DesignSpark and Pulsonix, fabricated through PCBway, and installed in DIN rail enclosures in the sugarhouse. The Elegoo Mars 3+ resin printer handles custom parts — component cartridges, brackets, fixtures. The Olympus SZ40 stereo microscope handles fine rework.

Graham handles hardware design questions and PCB review. Brant handles code review. Both have been involved in the SapMaster project.

The Sugarbush

Ancient Trees,
Modern Methods

The sugar maple is one of Eastern Ontario's greatest gifts — a tree that produces the sweetest sap in the world during the brief window between winter and spring, when freezing nights and warm days create the pressure that drives the flow.

Approximately 300 taps across 100 trees feed a vacuum-assisted collection system, pulling sap through lines into a central releaser tank.

From there, sap passes through reverse osmosis concentration before meeting the wood-fired evaporator — a continuous-boil system running all day, with syrup drawn only at day's end for a deep, caramel-rich, smoky character.

No shortcuts. No rush. The way it should be.

  • MEGA2560 + ESP32 Dual-processor architecture on a custom PCB. MEGA owns machine control, ESP32 owns HMI, WiFi, MQTT, and NTP.
  • Three Pump State Machines Vacuum pump with ball valve sequencing, transfer pump with watchdog, RO pump with duty cycle management.
  • Reverse Osmosis Four 400GPD elements concentrating sap before evaporation. Pressure-monitored, float-switch controlled.
  • Remote Monitoring MQTT over WiFi to Android tablets running MQTTdash. Monitor and control from anywhere on the property.
